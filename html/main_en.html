Chapter one Review

We learned that the Internet is a huge “network of networks” Billions of devices—phones, laptops, servers—are all linked through different access technologies like WiFi, fiber, or mobile data. What makes this work smoothly is the use of protocols such as TCP and HTTP. These protocols act like traffic rules, making sure messages are sent, received, and understood the same way everywhere.

Steps in sending data

  1. A host application creates a message.
  2. The message is broken into packets.
  3. Packets travel across access networks to the Internet core.
  4. Routers forward them toward the destination.
  5. The receiving host reassembles the packets.

key concepts learned

  • Internet as a “network of networks”
  • Hosts at the edge (clients and servers)
  • Access technologies: WiFi, DSL, 4G/5G
  • Protocols everywhere: TCP/IP, HTTP, Ethernet
  • Packet switching and routing
